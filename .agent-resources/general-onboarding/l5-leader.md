# L5 — Leader (Tech Lead / Engineering Manager)

> Team members who own process and people. Need *team dynamics and governance*.

## Characteristics

- Responsible for team outcomes, not just individual contributions
- Needs to understand how the team works, not just what the team builds
- Must quickly earn trust and avoid disrupting working patterns unnecessarily
- Balances technical decisions with people and process considerations
- May be joining to lead a team through a transition (growth, reorganization, new direction)

## Layer 2 — Level-Specific Onboarding Content

### Support Structure

- **Leadership peer**: Pair with another tech lead or engineering manager for organizational context and norms.
- **Team retrospective access**: Review the last 3-6 months of retrospective notes to understand team health patterns.
- **1:1 with every team member**: Schedule 30-minute introductory 1:1s with each team member within the first two weeks. Listen more than talk.

### Learning Path

| Week | Focus | Deliverable |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Team introductions, process observation, 1:1s | Team dynamics assessment (strengths, friction points, morale) |
| 2 | Process review, governance audit, metrics review | Process improvement shortlist (max 3 items, prioritized) |
| 3-4 | First process improvement, hiring/staffing review | One quick win implemented (visible team improvement) |
| 5-12 | Team development, strategic alignment, sustainable cadence | Team operating rhythm established |

### Material Format Preferences

- **Do**: Team health dashboards, DORA metrics history, retrospective summaries, escalation path documentation, hiring pipeline status, team skill matrix
- **Don't**: Detailed coding guides (they'll code-review to learn), individual module documentation (too granular for first weeks)

### Onboarding Focus Areas

- **Team composition and skills**: Who does what, skill gaps, growth aspirations, flight risks.
- **Process and ceremonies**: Sprint cadence, standup format, retrospective process, planning process. What's working and what's not.
- **Review and approval workflow**: Code review expectations, PR approval requirements, deployment gates.
- **Escalation paths**: When and how to escalate technical issues, people issues, cross-team conflicts.
- **Hiring context**: Open roles, hiring pipeline, interview process, onboarding pipeline for future hires.
- **Budget and vendor management**: Infrastructure costs, SaaS subscriptions, contractor relationships.

### Governance Areas to Audit

Within the first month, review and understand:

| Area | Key Questions |
|------|--------------|
| Code review | Who reviews what? Average turnaround? Quality of feedback? |
| Deployment | How often? Who approves? What's the rollback process? |
| Incident response | Who's on call? What's the SLA? How are postmortems run? |
| Technical debt | Is there an inventory? How is it prioritized against features? |
| Team development | Are there growth plans? Regular feedback? Learning budget? |

### AI-Assisted Development Guidance

- Use AI for **rapid codebase orientation**: Enough to review code and make architectural decisions, not necessarily to write code
- Use AI for **process documentation**: Generate or improve team process docs, runbooks, onboarding guides for future hires
- Establish team **AI usage norms**: What AI tools are sanctioned, how AI-generated code should be reviewed, what quality bar applies
- Use AI to **scale your review capacity**: Leverage `/check review` and sub-agents to maintain review quality as the team grows

### Onboarding Milestones

| Milestone | Target | Verification |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Team relationships | Week 2 | Completed 1:1s with all team members |
| Process understanding | Week 2 | Can describe current team cadence and pain points |
| First quick win | Month 1 | One process improvement implemented and acknowledged by team |
| Trust established | Month 2 | Team brings issues to you proactively |
| Sustainable cadence | Month 3 | Team operating rhythm reflects your leadership adjustments |
